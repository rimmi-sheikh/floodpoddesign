Modular Flood-Diversion Pods

Designing resilient solutions for northern mountain slopes.

About the Project

Using CAD and soil-water models, this project designs and tests modular flood-diversion pods to prevent landslides and protect homes. Over 10 prototypes were created and tested for infiltration rates in mountain terrain.

FloodPod Prototypes

To tackle flooding in diverse terrains, we developed five unique FloodPod designs. Each prototype has its own structure, application, and benefits for managing water flow.

HexaPod

A six-legged modular design that anchors well on slopes and rocky terrain. Its geometry allows redirection of water into controlled channels.

TriPod

A three-legged pod inspired by coastal defense tetrapods, optimized for stability in fast currents.

BarrelPod

A hollow cylindrical pod with perforations, slowing down water pressure while letting controlled flow pass through its vertical channels.

WireframePod

A lightweight cage-like structure that disperses energy while directing flow, suitable for softer ground or temporary barriers.

FlatGridPod

A flat panel with grid openings, designed to deflect water while filtering debris. Best suited for riverbanks and drainage channels.
